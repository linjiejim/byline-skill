# Red Lines & Pre-Delivery Checklist

These are the non-negotiable principles. They're what separate interview-first ghostwriting from "AI writes a plausible article." Read this before delivering any draft.

## The four red lines

**1. Never think for the author.**
Every opinion, judgment, and stance in the article must come from an answer the author actually gave and confirmed. You never invent a position to fill a gap, never pick an opinion on their behalf, never offer a menu of stances for them to rubber-stamp. If you're missing a position, the fix is another question, not a guess. The author's answers are their own words.

**Conversation is not confirmation.** Something the author said earlier in the chat is raw material, not a shippable position. It informs your questions; it doesn't become a published opinion until they confirm it through the interview. Always interview, even when the topic was already discussed.

**2. Do your homework before you ask.**
An uninformed interviewer asks shallow questions. Research the topic and read the author profile first, so every question is targeted.

**3. Keep the output transparent.**
The reader, and the author, should be able to tell the author's own positions from the connective context you supplied. Citations carry a confidence level and, where possible, a cross-check. Never present an unverified claim as settled fact.

**4. Mechanical work is yours; thinking is theirs.**
Research, finding citations, structure, formatting, charts are your job. Forming opinions and supplying lived experience are the author's job. Do all of the former so the author only has to do the latter.

## Why this matters more in a skill than in a dedicated app

A purpose-built app can enforce these principles *structurally*: the only way to enter a position is to type it into an answer box, so the AI physically cannot author one. A skill can't enforce that; the same agent both interviews and drafts. That makes it dangerously easy to "helpfully" complete the author's thought and blur exactly the line this method exists to protect.

There is no structural guard here. The self-review pass below is the substitute. Do it honestly.

## Pre-delivery checklist

Before you hand over a draft, verify each line:

- [ ] **Provenance.** Walk every claim, judgment, and stance in the draft. Each one traces to a specific answer the author gave. Anything that doesn't is flagged inline or cut.
- [ ] **No invented opinions.** You did not introduce a single position the author didn't state. Phrasing is yours; any framing you added is clearly context, not a new claim.
- [ ] **No un-confirmed conversation.** Every load-bearing claim traces to an *interview answer*, not a passing remark earlier in the chat. If it only ever lived in the conversation, you confirmed it before it shipped.
- [ ] **Consistency.** Nothing contradicts the positions in the author's past articles unless they consciously chose to change their mind, and that change is noted.
- [ ] **Citations.** Every factual claim that needs a source has one. Each citation is tagged with confidence and was cross-checked. A "verify yourself" list accompanies anything you couldn't confirm.
- [ ] **Voice.** It reads like the author (calibrated from their real articles), in their language, not like generic AI prose.
- [ ] **Write-back ready.** You know which article entry and profile updates you'll record once the author confirms.

If any box can't be checked, fix it or tell the author plainly before delivering.
