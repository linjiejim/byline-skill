# Drafting & Citations

This covers *voice and provenance*. The mechanical output format (headings, punctuation, language, length, citation block, closing section) lives in `profile/voice.md`. Draft straight into that format; don't write generic prose and reformat later.

## Drafting in the author's voice

Write the full draft yourself (that's the "ghost" in ghostwriter), but it must sound like the author, not like you.

- **Pull voice from the author's real past articles, not from a description.** Before drafting, read 2 to 3 in full (the exemplars named in `profile/work.md`, plus one adjacent to this topic) and match their rhythm: sentence length, how opinionated the first person is, the analogies they reach for, how they open and close. A voice you copied from real text beats any adjective list in `voice.md`.
- **Use the author's own words from the interview wherever they're vivid.** A good answer often contains the best sentence in the article verbatim, so keep it.
- **Structure is yours to impose:** a sharp thesis, three or four supporting moves, a close. The author gave you raw material and opinions; turning it into a shaped argument is your job.
- **Open with a concrete scene, number, or sharp question.** Earn the next paragraph. Avoid generic throat-clearing.

## Provenance: keep the byline honest

The reader should never be misled about whose judgment they're reading.

- **The author's positions** are the load-bearing claims. Keep them prominent and in their voice.
- **Your contributions** are context, transitions, background, and citations. Supporting material, never new opinions.
- While drafting, keep an internal map of which paragraph came from which answer. You'll need it for the self-review pass, and it's what lets you honestly say "every view here is yours."
- Remember the rule from `SKILL.md`: a point the author made only in passing in the conversation is **not yet a confirmed position**. If it's load-bearing in the draft, it should trace to an interview answer, not a chat aside.

## Citations: two tiers

The article goes out under the author's name, so a wrong citation is *their* reputational risk.

1. **Source every factual claim** that isn't common knowledge.
2. **Cross-check** each source; don't rely on a single unverified result, and never fabricate a source or a quote.
3. **Tag confidence** internally as you go:
   - `[verified]`: cross-checked against a primary or strong secondary source.
   - `[plausible]`: single reasonable source, not independently confirmed.
   - `[unverified]`: could not confirm.

Then split into two outputs:

- **Shipping citations** go in the article's references section (format in `profile/voice.md`). These are part of the article. Prefer `[verified]` sources here.
- **The private "verify yourself" list is for the author only, not in the article.** Include every `[plausible]`/`[unverified]` item, plus any number, date, name, or quote the author should personally confirm. Deliver it alongside the draft in Phase 5. Be upfront that AI citation-finding carries hallucination risk; this list is not optional.

## Cross-linking

Check `profile/work.md`. If the author has written something adjacent, link to it and build on it instead of re-explaining. This is what makes a body of work feel coherent rather than a pile of disconnected posts. Self-reference is a feature.

## Draft skeleton

A reliable default shape; adapt to the piece and the author's style. The exact heading names and references format come from `profile/voice.md`.

```
[Frontmatter or title, per profile/voice.md]

[Hook: a concrete scene, number, or tension taken from an answer.]

[The thesis, stated plainly. The one thing this article says.]

## [Supporting move 1: usually the author's strongest or most experiential point]
## [Supporting move 2]
## [Supporting move 3: often the counter-argument or limitation, handled honestly]

## [Closing section per profile/voice.md: the author's personal judgment]

## [References, if the piece cites sources]
```

The skeleton serves the argument, not the other way around. If an answer demands a different shape, follow the answer.
